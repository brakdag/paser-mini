**Last Updated:** [17/04/2026]

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to clearly define the functions, responsibilities, and scope of the **Staff** teams (Human Resources, Finance, Internal IT, Operations, Legal) within [Company Name].

Our goal is to ensure that support staff act as growth enablers, removing friction for the product and development teams while maintaining administrative efficiency and regulatory compliance.

## 2. Scope and Definitions

This policy applies to all employees performing support, administrative, or internal technical advisory roles.

- **Core Teams (Line):** Departments that create and deliver direct value to the customer (Software Engineering, Product Management, QA, UX/UI, Sales).
- **Staff Teams (Support):** Departments that provide the infrastructure, talent, and administrative support necessary for the core teams to operate (HR, Finance, Legal, Internal SysAdmin).

## 3. Rules of Engagement (Service Culture)

To maintain the agility expected in a software company, Staff teams will adhere to the following principles:

1.  **Internal Customer Focus:** Developers and product teams are the "customers" of the Staff teams.
2.  **Agility and Automation:** Automated processes and digital tools will be prioritized over manual bureaucracy.
3.  **Transparency:** Policies regarding expenses, hiring, and technical support must be public and easily accessible.
4.  **SLA Compliance:** Every staff department must adhere to defined response times to avoid delaying the software development lifecycle.

### [Job Title, e.g., Internal IT Analyst]

- **Role Mission:** To provide and maintain the technological infrastructure and security required for daily operations.
- **Key Responsibilities:**
  - Manage access controls, SaaS licenses, and hardware provisioning.
  - Implement and enforce information security policies.
  - Provide technical support to internal users.

## 5. Internal Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

To prevent bottlenecks, the following maximum response times are established:

| Service                              | Owner       | Response Time    |
| :----------------------------------- | :---------- | :--------------- |
| Grant access to repositories/tools   | Internal IT | 4 business hours |
| Emergency budget approvals           | Finance     | 24 hours         |
| Post approved technical job openings | HR          | 48 hours         |
| Legal review of vendor contracts     | Legal       | 3 business days  |

## 6. Conflict Resolution and Escalation

In the event of a disagreement between a Core team and a Staff team (e.g., rejection of a necessary tool purchase or a hiring dispute):

1.  **Level 1:** A mediation meeting between the leads of both teams.
2.  **Level 2:** If no agreement is reached, the issue is escalated to the [Chief Operating Officer / COO], who will make the final decision based on product impact and budget constraints.

## 7. Policy Review

This document will be reviewed annually to align roles with the evolution of our software infrastructure and the company's scaling needs.
